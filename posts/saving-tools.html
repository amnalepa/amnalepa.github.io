Saving

once you have set your goals, saving is simple if you have the right tools

High Interest Savings Account (HISA)

  • as the name says, it's a savings account that pays high interest
    • for example, EQ bank in Canada currently pays 2.3%, which is higher than the average bank
    • typically banks can afford to pay higher interest rates if they have fewer branches
    • note that some banks have promotional interest rates; Tangerine is famous for offering special rates to some of it's customers every so often
  • it is recommended to save 3-6 months of expenses in a HISA
  • this money can be kept here because it is liquid; you can take the money out any time
  • you can pay your bills using money from this account

Guaranteed Investment Certificate (GIC)

  • guarantees you get everything that you invested plus some
  • you need to lock your money in for a specified period of time
  • in return for locking in your money, you you receive a certain percentage on the money you invested
  • typically, the longer you lock your money in for, the larger the interest rate

Exchange Traded Fund (ETF)

  • one fund can contain many different stocks/bonds
  • are bought and traded like a stock
  • lost cost
    • for example, if you purchase ETFs in Questrade, you pay no commission
    • you pay a management fee, but it ranges from 0.05% to 1.29%

Mutual Fund

  • similar to ETFs, but are more actively managed (stocks are bought and sold by fund managers)
  • fees typically range from 1% to 2.5%

Automated Transfers

  • one of the best saving tools are automated transfer
  • the idea is that you're able to pay you future self first
