Emergency Financials

as they say, this pandemic is unprecedented, so hopefully you came here looking for specific financial tips

Business Continuity

  • businesses are using continuity plans to ensure operations and business functions aren't severely impacted
  • think of your family like a business; you need to continue mission critical operations so your family can make it through this tough time
  • obviously, feeding the family and ensuring everyone has a safe place to live is top of mind
  • hopefully you have your business continuity plan (an emergency plan; the recommended 3-6 months of expenses saved for emergencies)
  • if you don't have this prepared, don't worry! the government is providing lots of help

Critical Operations

  • critical operation #1: review government support resources and see which you can apply to
    • CERB
    • mortgage deferral
    • see the Support for Individuals section of Canada's COVID-19 Economic Response Plan

  • critical operation #2: apply for a job if you need to
    • many places of essential business are hiring
    • ex. grocery stores and food delivery services

  • critical operation #3: assess your current financial situation
    • figure out which debts you have to pay now
    • if you still have your job, start to save your 3-6 months of expenses
    • cut costs if you can; cancel netflix and spend your quarantine reading books and playing board games!
    • contact your lendors and explain your situation to find out if they can help

Good Situation

  • if you consider yourself to be in a good financial situations, see if there's any way you can help others
    • support local businesses that are still open, if possible
    • support COVID-19 relief efforts at canadahelps
    • save for your future; add to your investments, the market is on sale!
