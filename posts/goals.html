Goals

the goal is to account for all our goals, so we're not held back from achieving them due to financial reasons

Professional Development

  • this may involve going to conferences, networking events, etc.
  • may also involve some form of education with the aim of attaining a certificate, a degree, a diploma, etc.
  • attaining this education has
    • a direct cost (course fees)
    • an indirect cost (time spent not working)
  • educating yourself may be the best investment
  • set goals for your professional development, plan the costs and understand the potential impact on future earning potential

Family/Friends

  • let's be honest, relationships cost money (kids? dates? hosting friends? birthday gifts?)
  • we typically make most financial sacrifices for our family and friends
  • recognize this, and understand your relationship goals
  • what costs may be involved?

Retirement

  • do you want to retire early?
  • maybe you want to be able to maintain a similar lifestyle when you retire
  • you may not neet to know just yet, but having some ideas of what you'd like doesn't hurt
  • set some goals and you can always re-evaluate them
  • this will likely be one of your largest financial goals

Travel/Hobbies

  • recognize your hobbies and activies that makes you feel good, like travelling
  • hobbies have costs, which can add up when we're really passionate about something
  • some hobbies are more expensive than others
  • set goals related to your hobbies and account for the costs

Health / Exercise

  • set fitness and health related goals
  • for example, playing a sport and going to the gym once a week
  • consider the costs of a gym membership and joining a sports team
  • also consider the costs of healthy food and taking care of yourself
