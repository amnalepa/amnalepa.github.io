Savings for a Rainy Day

unprecedented emergency situations (like pandemics) are better handled with emergency savings (or as the personal finance community likes to call it, savings for a rainy day)

An Emergency Fund

  • financial planners recommend 3-6 months of expenses saved for emergencies
  • consider your situation and expenses you may need ex. maybe you can move back with your parents in the case of an emergency to save on rent
  • keep your money somewhere it can be liquid (ex. use a High Interest Savings Account)
  • the idea is to have a financial safety net to take care of unexpected emergencies
  • once you have this saved up, you won't have second thoughts when you add to your investments about whether or not you may need the money to be liquid
  • investments are typically less liquid and if you've invested all your money with little saved for emergencies, you may be forced to sell your investments at the worst possible time
  • besides, you shouldn't invest money you will need in the short term (expect that when you invest the money, it's locked up for at least 10 years)
  • this will also prevent you from taking on debt (ex. taking out a line of credit or worse, using credit cards which have very high interest rates)
