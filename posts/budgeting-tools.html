Budgeting Tools

the key is to find one that works for you

Mint

  • free!
  • connects to all your bank accounts and all your transactions are synced automatically
  • gives an overview of debts, assets, net worth and goals all on the home page
  • view all transactions in one place and input cash transactions to be even more accurate
  • create budgets to track in real time and see if you’re over budget
  • set financial goals and Mint helps make them smart goals by making you think through how much you’ll need to save each month to achieve the goal

YNAB

  • monthly cost
  • intended for transactions to be manually exported from bank accounts and uploaded
  • interesting budgeting methodology (give every dollar a job, age your money, etc.)
  • lots of content: podcasts, videos, docs, live classes
  • primarily meant for budgeting; doesn’t give an overview of financial situation

Mint & YNAB

  • visualize net worth
  • goal setting and tracking
  • insights about spending
  • transaction splitting
  • work across devices

Spreadsheets

  • why not go old school?
  • highly customizable, can do whatever works for you
  • allows you to keep your data to yourself
  • not as easy to use on mobile devices
  • using this method currently because of the customization allowing me to track other things all in one place such as my TFSA contributions and my work benefits usage
