Spending

figure out which tools are best for your spending

Cash/Debit

  • is cash still king?
  • only really places trying to pay less taxes accept "cash only"
  • however, cash gets the job done if you have it
  • though it is hard to keep track of cash spending, so maybe use a debit card instead

Credit

  • credit isn't for everyone
    • some people should try to avoid credit, period
    • if you like to spend money you don't have, don't get access to credit unless absolutely necessary
    • even if necessary, try to find ways to avoid it to prevent a debt cycle (continued borrowing, more debt, higher costs) you may not be able to escape
    • being able to get access to credit is extremely important for your personal finances, so try to become responsible first and then learn about the types of credit

  • personally, I love credit cards because I get cashback on my spending
    • I always have enough money in my account to pay of my card at any time
    • I recognize that it's much easier to spend money because of how easy it is to just tap
    • it's not worth spending more money to get more rewards (rewards are a tiny percentage of the spend amount)
    • however, rewards on spending can add up, and sometimes you have to spend the money anyways
    • spending using a credit card can make keeping track of what you spent your money on

  • a line of credit can be useful
    • you can open a line of credit, which typically has a lower interest than a credit card and not use it
    • you would have it open for emergency purposes, but you should still have an emergency fund
    • in the short term it affects your credit score due to a hard credit check
    • in the long term it may help your credit score because you have access to credit
    • that's assuming you use a small percentage of the total amount available and you make your payments on time
